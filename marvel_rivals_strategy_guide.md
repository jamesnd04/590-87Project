# Marvel Rivals: Competitive Strategy Guide

> Current as of Season 7 (March–April 2026). Based on Diamond+ meta unless noted.

---

## 1. Core Game Overview

### Roles

| Role           | Function              | Key Responsibility                                          |
| -------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vanguard**   | Frontline tank        | Absorb damage, peel for backline, initiate or anchor fights |
| **Duelist**    | Primary damage dealer | Secure eliminations, apply pressure, execute flanks         |
| **Strategist** | Support/healer        | Sustain allies, cycle defensive ultimates, disrupt dives    |

**Critical rule:** Never run fewer than **2 Strategists**. No single support can solo-heal a team in Marvel Rivals. Compositions that drop to 1 Strategist show win rates around 46%; triple-DPS lineups collapse below 35%. The statistically dominant baseline is **2-2-2** (2 Vanguards, 2 Duelists, 2 Strategists) with a ~53–54% win rate across millions of tracked matches.

### Team Composition and Synergy

- Marvel Rivals has **no role lock**, so teams can freely choose unconventional distributions.
- **Triple Strategist (1-2-3)** has emerged as a persistent meta threat, exploiting healing uptime and ultimate cycling.
- Team-Up abilities are **not optional power bonuses**—they are core to competitive power spikes. Teams that ignore them consistently underperform.
- Hero swapping mid-match is not only allowed but **expected** in competitive play. Adaptability is a key strategic skill.

## 2. Team Composition Archetypes

### Dive

**Definition:** Dive comps use high-mobility heroes to rapidly collapse on a single target—almost always a Strategist or out-of-position Duelist—eliminate them, and disengage before the enemy team can respond. Fight duration is measured in seconds.

**Playstyle:** Heroes bypass the frontline entirely via flanks or aerial approach. All dive heroes call a target simultaneously (coordination is mandatory), burst it down, then retreat. A clean dive creates a 6v5 before the enemy team reacts.

**Strengths:**

- Destroys poke comps that rely on backline safety
- Punishes poor positioning ruthlessly
- Generates extreme pressure even without kills (forces cooldown usage)

**Weaknesses:**

- Hard countered by anti-dive Vanguards (The Thing, Peni Parker)
- Requires voice comms or pre-established communication
- Loses to brawl/sustain comps if the dive doesn't secure the kill quickly
- Falls apart against triple-Strategist healing throughput

**Example Heroes:**

- _Vanguard:_ Hulk, Venom, Captain America
- _Duelist:_ Spider-Man, Iron Fist, Magik, Black Panther, Psylocke
- _Strategist:_ Luna Snow, Mantis (both have CC to protect divers or enable pick)

**When to Play Dive:**

- Enemy backline is playing isolated without peel
- Enemy team runs a poke comp (Hela, Hawkeye, Punisher)
- Maps with flank routes and vertical access (Hell's Heaven, Symbiotic Surface flanks)
- Your team has voice communication

---

### Poke

**Definition:** Poke comps win fights at range, picking off over-extended targets with burst or hitscan damage before ever committing to a full team fight. They siege objectives from distance and only push after gaining numerical advantage.

**Playstyle:** Magneto or Doctor Strange hold the frontline with barrier shields while Duelists (Hela, Hawkeye, Punisher, Namor) deal sustained damage from cover. Strategists stay tucked behind tanks. Play is methodical—force the enemy to push into your angles.

**Strengths:**

- Easy to coordinate (no voice chat required)
- Excellent on defense in Convoy/Convergence
- Safe playstyle with consistent output
- Shields deny entire phases of dive engage

**Weaknesses:**

- Hard countered by dive if the dive secures the healer before barriers are up
- Loses to brawl if the enemy forces a close-range team fight
- Requires consistent aim and discipline
- Less effective on small/indoor maps with no sightlines

**Example Heroes:**

- _Vanguard:_ Magneto, Doctor Strange, Groot
- _Duelist:_ Hela, Punisher, Hawkeye, Namor, Black Widow
- _Strategist:_ Luna Snow, Loki, Rocket Raccoon

**When to Play Poke:**

- Maps with long sightlines (Yggdrasil Path, open sections of Birnin T'Challa)
- Playing defense on Convoy or Convergence
- Enemy team lacks strong dive or mobility
- Your team lacks coordinated communication

---

### Brawl (Rush/Control)

**Definition:** Brawl comps stick together as a deathball unit and win through sustained close-range damage and healing throughput. They march onto objectives and refuse to let the enemy disengage. Variants include area-denial control comps (Peni + Groot) for Domination.

**Playstyle:** All six players stay grouped, cycling AoE healing and absorbing incoming damage together. Characters like Emma Frost, Thor, and Groot provide CC and walls. Rocket Raccoon's AoE healing feeds the whole deathball. The goal is to make it impossible to survive the sustained pressure.

**Strengths:**

- Extremely hard to dive (grouped play provides peel automatically)
- Devastates poke comps in close-quarters
- Powerful on Domination objectives
- Sustain outlasts enemy burst windows

**Weaknesses:**

- Vulnerable to AoE ultimates (the grouping that is a strength becomes a liability)
- Poke comps can keep the deathball at bay if they have adequate shields and range
- Requires everyone to resist the temptation to spread out

**Example Heroes:**

- _Vanguard:_ Thor, Emma Frost, Groot, Magneto (as anchor)
- _Duelist:_ Punisher, Winter Soldier, Storm (for speed buff), Scarlet Witch
- _Strategist:_ Rocket Raccoon (AoE heals), Jeff the Land Shark, Cloak & Dagger

**Area-Control Subtype (Domination):** Peni Parker + Groot + Bucky (hook) creates a "death zone" comp: Peni lays traps and mines, Groot's walls deny escape, Bucky pulls enemies into the nest. Especially strong on Domination maps.

**When to Play Brawl:**

- Domination maps with enclosed capture points
- Attacking on Convoy (staying together near the payload)
- Enemy team lacks AoE ultimates or strong sustain
- Your team lacks the coordination for dive

---

### Composition Matchup Dynamics

The three archetypes form a **loose rock-paper-scissors** triangle, but it's not absolute:

```
Dive → beats → Poke
Poke → beats → Brawl
Brawl → beats → Dive
```

**Key caveats:**

- Triple Strategist breaks this triangle by making brawl nearly unkillable when sustained correctly
- A well-executed dive can still beat brawl if the dive hits before the deathball groups up
- Poke beats brawl only when sightlines exist; on tight maps, brawl wins
- Anti-dive heroes (The Thing, Peni, Namor) hard-invert the dive vs. poke matchup

## 3. Team-Up Mechanics

### What Are Team-Ups?

Team-Ups are passive or active bonus abilities that activate when specific heroes are on the same team. Each Team-Up has one **Anchor Hero** (must be present; gains a stat boost) and one or more **Secondary Heroes** (gain a new ability or passive). As of Season 7, there are **24 active Team-Ups**. They are subject to change, addition, and removal each season.

**Why They Matter:** Team-Ups can constitute a ~10–25% power increase to a hero's effectiveness in the right context. In competitive play, building a comp around 1–2 strong Team-Ups is standard practice. At high elo, ignoring Team-Ups is actively exploitable by opponents.

**Passive vs. Active:** Some Team-Ups are permanent passive boosts (active as long as heroes are selected). Others grant a new ability with a cooldown timer that must be actively used.

## 4. Hero Synergies (Who Works Well Together)

### Sustain Cores (Triple Strategist Backbone)

**Gambit + Invisible Woman + Luna Snow / White Fox**

- Gambit and Invisible Woman produce high raw healing numbers even in neutral phases
- Luna Snow and White Fox's pairing (Blessing of the Kumiho) adds charm CC on top of already strong healing
- Result: A triple-support sustain core that outlasts most burst windows even post-ult nerf
- _Why it works:_ Cycling three defensive ultimates creates ~40–50 seconds of near-invulnerability windows. Even with Season 7's ult nerf slowing cycles, the raw healing throughput dominates neutral play.

### Shield + Poke Core

**Magneto + Doctor Strange + Hela + Punisher**

- Magneto's bubble absorbs infinite damage; Strange's shields create firing lanes
- Hela and Punisher's hitscan output is protected by the shield wall
- _Why it works:_ Snipers need safety to output damage; shields provide that safety while also denying enemy angles.

### Dive Core

**Venom + Spider-Man / Iron Fist + Luna Snow**

- Venom initiates with rope engage + Symbiote Shenanigans heal chain from Jeff
- Spidey or Iron Fist follow up for burst
- Luna Snow's CC (freeze) protects Venom from getting collapsed on
- _Why it works:_ Venom soaks follow-up damage while Jeff's tendrils maintain HP, letting the dive last longer than the enemy can respond.

### Flying Meta / Aerial Comp

**Hulk + Iron Man (Gamma Charge) + Human Torch + Storm**

- Introduced to dominance in Season 1.5; still viable in Season 7
- Hulk/Iron Man combo enables vertical space control
- Storm provides speed and AoE
- _Weakness:_ Hitscan-heavy comps (Hela, Punisher) with shield tanks (Strange, Magneto) hard-counter this

### Area Control Core (Domination)

**Peni Parker + Groot + Rocket Raccoon**

- Peni's nest + mines transform the capture point into a death zone
- Groot's walls deny escape routes
- Rocket's AoE healing sustains the entire unit
- **Season 7 bonus:** Peni-Rocket Team-Up adds an extra Nest and a Rez Beacon that spawns mines

### Dive Enabler Patterns

- **Hulk as dive tank:** Leap initiates, heavy CC brings enemy out of position, strong HP pool enables sustained engagement
- **Mantis + dive DPS:** Mantis's offensive healing keeps divers alive mid-engage; her CC stun bails out failed dives
- **Loki in dive:** Loki clones confuse peel attempts; teleport creates escape tools for divers

## 5. Counters and Anti-Synergies

### The General Counter Rule for Duelists

> **Poke counters Brawl → Brawl counters Dive → Dive counters Poke**

This applies within the Duelist class as well as between comp archetypes.

---

### Anti-Dive Picks

The most important category in the current meta given dive's popularity.

| Hero                 | Role       | Why They Counter Dive                                                                                                                      |
| -------------------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **The Thing**        | Vanguard   | Embattled Leap + Haymaker interrupts mobility ability animations; wins most 1v1s against dive Vanguards; Slam locks out movement abilities |
| **Peni Parker**      | Vanguard   | Nest + mines create a death zone; Spider-Man and Iron Fist literally cannot safely approach nests; space denial is passive                 |
| **Namor**            | Duelist    | Auto-detecting squid summons reveal and punish flankers; Water Ball one-shots most dive DPS                                                |
| **Emma Frost**       | Vanguard   | Diamond form is immune to displacement; Chokeslam punishes diving Vanguards like Wolverine; massive CC kit                                 |
| **White Fox**        | Strategist | Kumiho form is an active anti-dive tool; dashes and teleport let her survive melee divers; charm CC disrupts combos                        |
| **Luna Snow**        | Strategist | Freeze stun on 15s CD; Ultimate saves the whole team from a dive wipe                                                                      |
| **Mantis**           | Strategist | Sleep/stun punishes aggressive divers; offensive healing lets the team burst the diver instead                                             |
| **Invisible Woman**  | Strategist | Push/pull displaces Wolverine's leap animation, Venom's engage, Angela's dive; hard counters displacement-reliant divers                   |
| **Mister Fantastic** | Duelist    | Can absorb burst that would one-shot other heroes; menace against backline divers                                                          |

**Practical anti-dive approach:** Against triple-dive (e.g., Black Panther + Magik + Psylocke), run Peni as tank, Namor + one other DPS, and CC supports (Mantis + Luna). Group together to deny isolated picks.

---

### Anti-Poke Picks

| Hero                | Role     | Why They Counter Poke                                                                             |
| ------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Spider-Man**      | Duelist  | Can close range gaps instantly; difficult to track; forces Hawkeye/Hela off sightlines            |
| **Iron Fist**       | Duelist  | Lock-on tracking closes distance; block absorbs key cooldowns; nightmare for isolated poke heroes |
| **Venom**           | Vanguard | Dives over shield tanks to reach poke backline                                                    |
| **Captain America** | Vanguard | Can't be stopped by most CC; deflects ultimates; forces poke players into unfavorable positions   |

**Key insight:** Dive into poke requires hitting the Strategist BEFORE the shield tank can respond. The barrier Vanguards (Magneto, Strange) are specifically hard counters to dive—so against poke with shields, the dive needs to go around, not through.

---

### Anti-Brawl Picks

| Hero / Strategy                                       | Why It Works                                                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **AoE ultimates** (Moon Knight, Namor, Scarlet Witch) | Grouped deathballs are maximally vulnerable to AoE                                           |
| **Hela + Punisher hitscan**                           | Sustained ranged output from safety; brawl has to push through damage to reach poke backline |
| **Long-range maps / open sightlines**                 | Brawl comps lose the ability to group safely under sustained sniper fire                     |
| **Shield tanks (Magneto + Strange)**                  | Keep brawl at range; deny the close-quarters engagement brawl needs                          |

---

### Notable Specific Counters

- **Magneto / Doctor Strange** are countered by **Iron Fist, The Thing, Hulk, Angela** (melee breaks through shields)
- **Wolverine** is countered by **Emma Frost** (chokeslam), **Invisible Woman** (push/pull disrupts leap), **Captain America** (only Vanguard with a favorable Wolverine matchup)
- **Hawkeye** is countered by coordinated dive (Spider-Man, Iron Fist closing angles)
- **Angela** is countered by hitscan (Punisher, Hawkeye) and CC supports (Luna Snow freeze, Invisible Woman push)
- **Fliers (Iron Man, Storm, Human Torch)** are countered by hitscan Duelists (Hela, Punisher) and shield tanks that deny aerial angles

- **Rocket Raccoon** – AoE healing suits any grouped comp

## 7. Drafting & Team Building Guidelines

### The Baseline: Start with Role Structure

1. **Lock 2 Strategists first.** This is non-negotiable. The win rate data is unambiguous. Pick supports that cover both burst and sustain healing where possible (e.g., Mantis for burst + Rocket for sustain).
2. **Identify your comp type** based on map (open = poke, tight = brawl, flank routes available = dive).
3. **Select Vanguards that match your comp.** Shield tanks for poke; dive tanks for dive; brawl tanks for control.
4. **Fill Duelists last**, choosing heroes that fit the comp and exploit expected enemy weaknesses.

---

### When to Prioritize Team-Ups

Prioritize a Team-Up when:

- The anchor hero is already a must-pick for your comp (e.g., Venom in dive → take Jeff for Symbiote Shenanigans automatically)
- The Team-Up's secondary heroes are close to equal in value with alternatives
- The team-up provides a clear power spike (Mantis's Life and Death, Arcane Order, Psionic Mayhem)

Do **not** force a Team-Up when:

- It requires running two heroes that fill redundant roles (Blade + Moon Knight both as DPS)
- The anchor hero is weak in the current meta
- It sacrifices composition balance (e.g., running a third DPS just for a Team-Up)

---

### When to Prioritize Counter-Picks

- If enemy runs a dominant dive comp → swap to The Thing or Peni + Namor immediately
- If enemy poke is winning fights → swap a Vanguard to a dive initiator (Venom, Hulk) and pressure the backline
- If enemy triple-support is out-healing all damage → consider burst ultimates and heroes with anti-heal (Gambit's anti-heal application)
- Maintain at least **1–2 flex heroes** who can adapt to unexpected enemy picks

---

### Decision-Making Framework

```
1. What map/mode are we on?
   └─ Open sightlines → default Poke
   └─ Enclosed/Domination → Brawl or Area Control
   └─ Flank routes available → evaluate Dive

2. What did the enemy pick?
   └─ Heavy dive → get The Thing / Peni / Namor
   └─ Poke backline → get dive or mobility DPS
   └─ Triple support → get burst damage ultimates

3. Do we have a strong Team-Up available?
   └─ Anchor hero is already in comp → take the Team-Up
   └─ Otherwise → don't bend the comp to force it

4. Are we missing anything critical?
   └─ Solo support? → swap a DPS for a second Strategist immediately
   └─ No peel for backline? → add CC support (Mantis, Luna, Invisible Woman)
   └─ No tank? → you will lose to any coordinated push

5. Can we swap mid-match?
   └─ Yes. Always swap if a hero is being hard-countered.
   └─ Never stay on a hero that has zero impact—swapping is not "giving up."
```

---

### Common Drafting Mistakes

| Mistake                                 | Why It Loses                                           | Fix                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Solo Strategist                         | No healing redundancy; one elimination = team collapse | Always 2 Strategists minimum                                                              |
| Triple Duelist (1-3-2)                  | Low win rate above Gold; can't sustain through fights  | Drop a DPS for a tank                                                                     |
| Forcing three tanks (3-0-3)             | No damage output; can't secure kills                   | At least 1–2 Duelists                                                                     |
| Static comp, never swapping             | Gets hard-countered and stays there                    | Swap aggressively when countered                                                          |
| Ignoring Team-Ups entirely              | Forfeits free power spikes                             | At least acknowledge anchor hero bonuses                                                  |
| Two Strategists with only sustain heals | No defensive ultimates → instant team wipes            | Include at least 1 Strategist with a defensive ultimate (Luna Snow, Adam Warlock, Mantis) |
